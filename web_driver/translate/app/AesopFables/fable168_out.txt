Fable 168

The Murderer(謀殺犯) and the Nile(尼羅河)

A man had committed(犯罪) a murder and was being pursued追捕) by the victim’s relatives. He reached the river Nile, and when he found a lion there he was afraid and climbed up a tree; in the tree, he saw a snake and was practically(差不多) scared to death, so he threw himself into the river, where a crocodile(鱷魚) devoured(吞食) him.The story is for people who commit murders: neither(（既不……也不) earth nor air nor water nor any other place will be able to protect them.

NOTE : Other versions(版本) of this fable (included in Chambry’s first edition of the Greek fables) feature(以…為特色) a wolf instead of a lion.
